# HR Analytics & Employee Attrition Dashboard (Power BI)

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, and organizational factors that may influence employee turnover.

The dashboard helps HR professionals and business leaders understand workforce trends, identify potential attrition drivers, and support data-driven decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- Monitor employee attrition across the organization.
- Identify factors associated with employee turnover.
- Analyze workforce demographics and employee characteristics.
- Support HR decision-making through data visualization and insights.

---

## 📊 Dashboard Structure

### 1. Executive Summary

Provides a high-level overview of key HR metrics and workforce composition.

**Key Metrics**
- Total Employees
- Attrition Rate
- Average Monthly Income
- Average Job Satisfaction
- Average Work-Life Balance

**Analysis**
- Employee Distribution by Attrition Status
- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition by Gender
- Attrition by Marital Status

---

### 2. Attrition Drivers Analysis

Explores workplace factors that may influence employee turnover.

**Analysis**
- Attrition by Overtime
- Attrition by Business Travel
- Attrition by Distance from Home
- Attrition by Job Satisfaction
- Attrition by Environment Satisfaction
- Attrition by Work-Life Balance

---

### 3. Workforce Profile

Provides insights into workforce demographics and employee characteristics.

**Analysis**
- Employee Distribution by Age Group
- Employee Distribution by Income Group
- Employee Distribution by Experience Level
- Employee Distribution by Years at Company
- Employee Distribution by Job Level
- Employee Distribution by Education Field

---

## 📈 Key Insights

Based on the dashboard analysis:

- The organization has an attrition rate of approximately 16%.
- Research & Development represents the largest department.
- Sales Executive is the most common job role.
- Most employees fall within the 30–39 age group.
- A significant portion of employees have 6–10 years of total work experience.
